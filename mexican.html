Mexican Ceviche

prep time

3 hrs 20 min

Servings

4

instructions

step 1

In a large bowl, combine the shrimp, red onion, tomatoes, jalapeno, cilantro, lime juice, lemon juice, and salt.

step 2

Stir well and cover with plastic wrap. Refrigerate for 2-3 hours or until the shrimp is fully cooked and turns opaque.

step 3

Stir in the diced avocado and serve with corn tortilla chips.

ingredients

1 lb raw large shrimp, peeled,deveined, and chopped

1/2 medium red onion, finely chopped

2 medium ripe tomatoes, seeded and diced

1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped

1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped

2 limes, juiced

1 lemon, juiced

1/4 teaspoon salt

2 avocados, peeled and diced

tortilla chips

Mexican Lasagna

prep time

15 min.

cook time

45 min

servings

5

instructions

step 1

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).

step 2

In a large skillet, cook ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until beef is browned and onion is soft, about 5-7 minutes.

step 3

Stir in the diced tomatoes, green chilies, chili powder, cumin, and salt. Cook for another 5 minutes or until mixture is heated through.

step 4

Layer 3-4 tortillas in the bottom of an 8x8 inch baking dish. Spread 1/3 of the refried beans over the tortillas.

step 5

Spread 1/3 of the beef mixture over the beans, then sprinkle with 1/3 of the cheese. Repeat layering with tortillas, beans, beef mixture, and cheese, two more times.

step 6

Cover with foil and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.

step 7

Get lasagna out of the oven and let cool for 10 minutes prior serving.

ingredients

1 lb ground beef

1 small onion, diced

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes

1 can (4 oz) diced green chilies

1 tsp. chili powder

1 tsp. cumin

Salt, to taste

9-10 corn tortillas

2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend, divided

1 can (15 oz) refried beans

2 tbsp. chopped fresh cilantro, optional

Salsa and sour cream, for serving (optional)

Mexican Cassarole

prep time

30 min

cook time

45 hr

Servings

6

instructions

step 1

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).

step 2

In a large skillet, cook ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until beef is browned and onion is soft, about 5-7 minutes.

step 3

Drain any excess grease, then stir in the chili powder, cumin, salt, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, and salsa.

step 4

In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread half of the beef mixture over the bottom.

step 5

Arrange 4 of the tortillas over the beef mixture.

step 6

Sprinkle half of the cheese on top of the tortillas, then spread half of the black beans over the cheese.

step 7

Repeat the layers with the remaining beef mixture, tortillas, cheese, and black beans.

step 8

Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

step 9

Serve topped with sour cream.

ingredients

1 lb ground beef

1 small onion, diced

1 green bell pepper, diced

3 cloves garlic, minced

2 tsp chili powder

1 tsp salt

1 tsp. cumin

1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes

1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce

1/2 cup salsa

8 corn tortillas

2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend

1 can (15 oz) black beans

1/2 cup sour cream

Mexican Shepherd's Pie

prep time

30 min

cook time

1 hour

Servings

6

instructions

step 1

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

step 2

Put the the ground beef or turkey in a large skillet over the stove in medium heat and brown it. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the large skillet and cook until the onion is translucent.

step 3

Stir in the diced tomatoes, corn, black beans, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt and pepper and cook for a few minutes.

step 4

Transfer the beef mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish.

step 5

Spoon the mashed potatoes over the beef mixture, spreading it evenly.

step 6

Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese over the top.

step 7

Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the edges are golden brown.

step 8

Serve hot.

ingredients

1 lb ground beef or ground turkey

1 onion, diced

3 cloves of garlic, minced

1 can of diced tomatoes

1 can of corn, drained

1 can of black beans

1 tbsp. chili powder

1 tsp. cumin

1 tsp. paprika

Salt and pepper, to taste

4 cups of mashed potatoes

1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese

Mexican Succotash

prep time

15 min.

cook time

25 min

Servings

6

instructions

step 1

Put olive oil in a large skillet and use medium heat. Put the turkey in the large skillet and stir it until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes.

step 2

Add the onion and bell peppers and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until softened.

step 3

Add the garlic and cook for another minute.

step 4

Stir in the corn, black beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until heated through.

step 5

Serve the succotash hot, garnished with fresh cilantro, if desired.

ingredients

1 pound ground turkey

1 tbsp olive oil

1 medium onion, diced

1 red bell pepper, diced

1 yellow or green bell pepper, diced

2 cloves garlic, minced

2 cups fresh or frozen corn kernels

1 tsp chili powder

1 tsp ground cumin

Salt and pepper, to taste

Fresh cilantro, for garnish
